What Hexago Warehousing Includes

Hexago positions warehousing as a working operational layer between inbound receipt and outbound dispatch. The focus is on practical storage, dependable movement control, and better inventory discipline.

  • Inbound unloading and stock intake coordination
  • Storage planning for active and reserve inventory
  • Pick, sort, staging, and dispatch preparation support
  • Integration with transport and supply chain movement

Who It Is Built For

Businesses that are growing beyond informal stock handling usually benefit most from structured 3PL warehousing.

  • Distributors and wholesalers managing regional inventory
  • E-commerce and omnichannel brands needing fast order readiness
  • Importers and manufacturers needing buffer stock and dispatch control
  • Companies that want a Kerala hub without building in-house warehousing from scratch
If your stock is moving often, the warehouse should improve control, picking discipline, and dispatch speed instead of acting as passive storage space.

Warehousing Frequently Asked Questions

Practical answers for storage and dispatch planning.

Does Hexago only provide storage?

No. The warehousing offer is more useful when storage is linked with handling, dispatch, and transport coordination.

Can the warehouse support regular outbound dispatches?

Yes. The model is intended to support stock readiness, staging, and structured outbound movement for repeat operations.

Is this suitable for e-commerce and distribution businesses?

Yes. Brands and distributors that need recurring stock movement can use the warehouse as an active operational node.

How do I start planning storage with Hexago?

Share your approximate stock profile, dispatch frequency, and handling needs. Hexago can then shape the warehousing setup around the operating requirement.
