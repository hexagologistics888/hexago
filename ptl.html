What Partial Truck Load Means

PTL, also called shared truck movement, allows your cargo to travel with other compatible consignments on the same route. It is a strong option for loads that are too large for courier movement but too small for a dedicated truck.

  • Practical for smaller or medium-volume freight
  • Useful for regular, scheduled dispatches
  • Well suited to cost-sensitive logistics planning

When PTL Is The Better Choice

PTL works best when the business needs stable dispatch and predictable freight handling without paying FTL rates on every shipment.

  • You dispatch in smaller batches or mixed order cycles
  • Some transit flexibility is acceptable
  • The freight does not require a dedicated vehicle
  • Cost optimization is part of the shipping decision
For many distributors and fast-growing brands, PTL is the bridge between small parcel movement and full truck deployment.

PTL Vs FTL

Both models are useful. The choice depends on shipment profile and delivery expectations.

Choose PTL When

  • Your shipment only needs part of a truck
  • You want lower transport cost per dispatch
  • The route can operate through planned consolidation
  • You ship frequently in smaller volumes

Choose FTL When

  • The cargo needs dedicated truck space
  • Transit urgency is higher
  • The freight should avoid shared handling
  • The load size already justifies full capacity

PTL Frequently Asked Questions

Common decisions buyers ask before moving shared freight.

What is the usual PTL shipment range?

PTL is generally used when cargo is too substantial for parcel movement but not large enough to justify a dedicated truck. Hexa-go already positions PTL for smaller freight and mid-volume dispatches.

Will PTL always be cheaper than FTL?

Not always, but PTL is commonly the more efficient option when the load only needs part of a truck and dedicated capacity is unnecessary.

Can PTL be used for recurring monthly routes?

Yes. PTL is often a good fit for steady recurring movement where shipment volumes are consistent but below full truck threshold.

Is tracking available on PTL movement?

Yes. Hexa-go provides shipment updates and operational coordination so delivery planning remains visible to the customer team.
