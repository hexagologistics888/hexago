What Full Truck Load Means

Full Truck Load means your freight occupies a dedicated vehicle for the route. Instead of sharing truck space, the shipment moves as one controlled job from origin to destination.

This model is useful when businesses need stronger schedule control, direct movement, and less cargo handling across the journey.

  • Best fit for larger or denser consignments
  • Preferred for planned dispatches and contract movement
  • Supports fixed delivery windows and plant-to-plant flow

FTL Vs PTL

Choose the model based on volume, urgency, and how much control the shipment needs.

Choose FTL When

  • Your load needs a full truck or dedicated route
  • Transit speed matters more than sharing cost
  • Cargo should avoid cross-docking and consolidation touches
  • You need better control over dispatch and arrival windows

PTL May Be Better When

  • The shipment volume is smaller and cost efficiency matters most
  • Some additional transit time is acceptable
  • You want to pay only for the portion of truck space you use
  • The load is routine and not highly time-critical

FTL Frequently Asked Questions

Key answers for teams evaluating dedicated truck movement.

What shipment size usually justifies FTL?

FTL is normally evaluated when volume, weight, urgency, or cargo sensitivity makes a dedicated vehicle more practical than shared capacity.

Does FTL reduce the risk of damage?

Yes. Because the freight stays in one truck with fewer loading cycles, the cargo usually faces less manual handling across the trip.

Can Hexa-go support recurring lanes?

Yes. Repeat interstate movement and planned dispatch lanes can be structured as a regular operating flow with coordinated truck allocation.

Is tracking available during movement?

Hexa-go provides shipment visibility and operational updates so teams can plan receiving and downstream dispatch more accurately.
